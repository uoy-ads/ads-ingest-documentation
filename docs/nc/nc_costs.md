### Explanation of fees

We are committed to providing open and free access to data. We therefore impose a one-off payment for deposition to safeguard the long-term future of the digital data.

Below is an explanation of the fees shown on the Costs page and what they relate to.

* __Object Fee__ - A fee is applied per digital object based on data category and file format. Some digital objects are charged on a sliding scale so the more you archive, the lower the cost per object. For some file types (e.g. photographs), a set number of files can be deposited for free under the general Accession Fee (discussed below). If this is the case the word ‘Free’ is shown under Cost.  
* __Accession Fee__ - This is a standard fee that we apply to all depositions and includes a standard interface for your collection.  
* __Storage Fee__ - This fee relates to the cost we charge for the ongoing curation of a collection.  The fee is calculated per GB of uploaded data with the first 100GB included for free as part of the accession fee.

### Download a copy

To download a copy of your quotation click the ‘Download’ button on the top right hand corner of the page. This will allow you to download a pdf version of the costings calculated on this page that you can share with your organisation.

This cost estimate assumes that the data and documentation in this collection has been prepared in accordance with our [Instructions for Depositors](https://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/help-guidance/instructions-for-depositors/) and is accompanied by appropriate metadata. Changes to data after submission may incur additional charges as will requests for special collection interface requirements. If you have any queries or questions regarding these costs please [contact us](https://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/contact/).
